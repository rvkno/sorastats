## Types of Sora Video Alerts

SoraStats offers four categories of alerts, each serving a distinct strategic purpose. Understanding when and how to use each type is key to avoiding alert fatigue while catching real opportunities.

### Momentum Spike Alerts

These trigger when any video (yours or others') experiences explosive growth beyond normal patterns. The default threshold is +500% views in 24 hours, but you can customize based on your niche.

**What you get:** Email notification with video details, current view count, engagement metrics, and topic analysis.

**Use case:** A video about "AI-generated holiday cards" jumps from 3,000 to 30,000 views overnight. Your alert fires at 6 AM. By 10 AM, you've created and published your own holiday card tutorial, capturing secondary traffic while the topic trends.

**Best for:** Trend-focused creators who want to ride viral waves in their niche.

### Rank Change Alerts

Notifications when your creator ranking moves significantly up or down. Set thresholds like "Alert if I drop below Top 500" or "Notify when I break Top 100."

**What you get:** Your new rank, previous rank, change magnitude, and which video(s) drove the shift.

**Use case:** You've hovered around #520 for weeks. Overnight, a video goes viral and you jump to #380. The alert confirms your breakout moment, letting you immediately double down on that content format.

**Best for:** Growth-focused creators tracking competitive positioning.

### Follower Velocity Alerts

Detect sudden acceleration in follower growth rate. If your typical daily gain is 50 followers and you suddenly add 400 in 24 hours, the system flags this anomaly.

**What you get:** Current follower count, growth rate, comparison to your average, and likely causes (viral video, external mention, algorithm boost).

**Use case:** Your follower growth triples overnight. The alert prompts you to identify the trigger (a viral video, external share, or algorithmic promotion) so you can replicate the pattern.

**Best for:** Creators focused on audience building and understanding what drives follows versus just views.

### Competitor Watch Alerts

Track specific creators and receive notifications when they publish high-performing content.

**What you get:** Notification when a watched creator's video exceeds your defined threshold (e.g., 50,000 views), with direct link to their content.

**Use case:** A competitor in your niche suddenly achieves 120,000 views on a tutorial. You receive the alert within 4 hours, analyze their approach, identify what they didn't cover, and create a complementary piece that captures related searches.

**Best for:** Creators in competitive niches who want market intelligence without constant manual checking.

## Configuring Alert Thresholds: Best Practices

Badly configured alerts create noise. Well-tuned alerts deliver actionable intelligence.

### Start Conservative, Then Adjust

Don't set every threshold to the most sensitive settings. Begin with these proven baselines:

**Momentum Spike:**

* 500% view increase in 24 hours (catches true breakouts without daily noise)
* Minimum 1,000 baseline views (filters out random spikes on tiny videos)

**Rank Change:**

* Movement of 50+ positions (smaller changes are often just algorithmic fluctuations)
* Daily maximum (prevents alert fatigue from hourly rank volatility)

**Follower Velocity:**

* 2x your daily average gain (if you average 50 followers/day, alert at 100+)
* 24-hour window (shorter windows create too many false positives)

**Competitor Watch:**

* Videos exceeding 50,000 views (adjust based on niche - some niches rarely hit this)
* 48-hour window (focuses on recent, relevant content)

### The One-Week Test

After setting up alerts, track for one week:

* How many alerts fired?
* How many were actionable (led to a content decision or strategy adjustment)?
* How many were noise (interesting but not useful)?

Adjust thresholds to achieve a 70%+ actionability rate. If you're getting 20 alerts per day but only act on 3, tighten your thresholds.

## Alert Response Playbook

Receiving an alert is step one. Having a prepared response plan is what separates growth from notification fatigue.

### When Momentum Spike Alert Fires

**Within 30 minutes:**

* Open the viral video
* Analyze: Hook (first 2 seconds), format, emotional angle, topic
* Screenshot or save the prompt if it's remixable

**Within 2 hours:**

* Brainstorm 2-3 variations that connect the trend to your niche
* Don't copy - add your unique perspective or fill a gap they missed

**Within 12 hours:**

* Create and publish your response content
* Reference the trend in your caption to catch related searches

**Why speed matters:** Viral content has a 24-48 hour primary wave. Publishing your version within 12 hours catches the secondary wave.

### When Rank Change Alert Fires

**If you jumped up:**

* Identify which video caused the spike (check last 24-48 hours)
* Analyze what made it successful (format, topic, timing, hook)
* Create 2-3 similar videos immediately to compound momentum
* Update your content calendar to prioritize this format

**If you dropped:**

* Review your last 5 videos for performance declines
* Check if a competitor in your niche had a breakout (use Competitive Intelligence)
* Assess whether you've been posting less frequently
* Adjust strategy: increase posting cadence or improve content quality

### When Follower Velocity Alert Fires

**Immediate actions:**

* Check which video attracted the followers (highest views in last 24 hours)
* Review traffic sources (did external platform drive traffic?)
* Look for external mentions (someone shared your content on Twitter, Reddit, etc.)

**Within 48 hours:**

* Engage with new followers (reply to their comments to build loyalty)
* Create follow-up content similar to what attracted them
* Consider posting a "welcome" video acknowledging new followers

### When Competitor Watch Alert Fires

**Within 1 hour:**

* Watch the competitor's viral video
* Take notes: format, pacing, hooks, unique angles

**Within 4 hours:**

* Identify what they didn't cover (most viral videos leave gaps)
* Check if the topic has room for multiple perspectives (most do)

**Within 24 hours:**

* Create a complementary piece that adds value beyond their video
* Use related but differentiated keywords to capture parallel searches

## Advanced Alert Strategies

### Layered Alert Combinations

Advanced creators stack alerts to catch complex patterns:

**Example: Trend Prediction Stack**

* Momentum Spike alert for your niche keywords
* Competitor Watch on 3 trend-setting creators
* Follower Velocity to catch when your response videos resonate

When all three fire within 48 hours, it signals a major trend wave you're successfully riding.

### Seasonal Alert Adjustments

Trends behave differently across seasons:

**Holiday periods (Nov-Dec):** Lower momentum spike thresholds (300% vs 500%) because baseline engagement rises platform-wide.

**Summer (Jun-Aug):** Tighten thresholds (700% vs 500%) because overall activity is lower, making relative spikes less meaningful.

**Platform anniversaries:** Expect rank volatility; temporarily disable rank change alerts to avoid noise.

### Geographic Alert Routing

If you collaborate with others or manage multiple accounts:

**Use case:** You focus on US trends, your collaborator tracks Asian markets.

**Setup:** Create separate alerts filtered by time zones or regional keywords, routing notifications to different email addresses.

## Common Alert Setup Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Setting Thresholds Too Sensitive

Receiving 50 alerts per day creates decision paralysis. You stop checking them, defeating the purpose.

**Fix:** Start conservative (500% momentum threshold, 50-position rank changes). After one week, tighten only if you're missing opportunities.

### Mistake 2: Not Creating a Dedicated Workflow

Alerts buried in your main inbox get ignored.

**Fix:** Set up a Gmail filter or dedicated Slack channel for SoraStats alerts. Treat alerts as priority tasks requiring 1-4 hour response windows.

### Mistake 3: Watching Too Many Competitors

Tracking 20 creators generates constant noise.

**Fix:** Watch only 3-5 creators who are 1-2 tiers above your current position and directly relevant to your niche.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Alert Performance Review

What worked in month one may not work in month three as your channel evolves.

**Fix:** Monthly, review your alert log. Calculate actionability rate (actionable alerts / total alerts). Adjust thresholds to maintain 60-70% actionability.
